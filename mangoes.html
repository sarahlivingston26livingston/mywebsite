The Beauty of Mangoes


 

  • The flowers of a mango tree are small and white with five petals, and the fruit takes between three and six months to ripen.
    • More fresh mangoes are eaten around the world every day than any other fruit.
  • Mango salsa and chutney are very popular as accompaniments to salads, chicken, and fish or served as a snack.
  • Giving someone a basket of mangoes is considered a gesture of friendship.
  • Mango fruit can come in various shapes, size and colour including yellow, orange, red and green.

Health Beneifits

  1. Mangoes are a good source of antioxidants, containing certain phytochemicals such as gallotannins and mangiferin which have been studied for their health benefits.
  2. Just 80g of mango (2 x 2 inch slices) counts as one of your five-a-day. This one portion will provide 53 calories, 11g of naturally-occurring sugar and just over 2g of fibre.
  3. Mango is a low-calorie fruit that is high in fibre, and is a great source of vitamins A and C. It also contains folate, B6, iron and a little calcium, zinc and vitamin E.
  4. Mango leaves are filled with remedial and curative attributes. They are rich in vitamins, minerals, nutrients, and antioxidants, which help in treating or preventing diabetes, blood pressure, restlessness, gall and kidney stones, respiratory problems, and dysentery.

Interesting Facts; 

In 2010, Bangladesh declared the Mango tree as its National Tree. Earlier, Bangladesh had no National Tree.
